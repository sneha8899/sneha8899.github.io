Undergraduate Teaching Assistant, The Ohio State University College of Engineering

August 2018 - Present

Engineering and technology is something that I am really passionate about, and I also really enjoy sharing that sentiment with other people. As a UTA for the College of Engineering, I work with 66 freshman. I assist a lead instructor with teaching the class, which is The Fundamentals of Engineering, an introductory engineering course. This class teaches basic engineering skills and techniques.

In addition, I help with grading all of the assignments, and spend time in and out of the classroom helping the students. This has really strengthened my time management and problem solving skills. I have to make sure that I can devote enough time to my schoolwork as well as their school work. On top of that, I try to make myself available outside of the classroom - either by email or face to face conversations to help with any issues that may arise. In terms of my problem-solving skills, there are a lot of strange problems and errors that come up in their work, and I have had to really learn how to think around them or how to fix them. This has really forced me to expand the way I think and problem solve.

Finally, being a UTA has improved both my leadership and team work skills. As someone these students look up to, I have to make sure that I am a good role model, but also that I can demonstrate the ability to do the work that they're doing. In terms of team work, being a UTA is an interesting position to be in. Not only do I have to have the answers, but I have to lead them to the answers, or maybe just be there to bounce ideas off of.

Overall, being a UTA has really made me grow as a person, and also understand better how to work with and for other people.
