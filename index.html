Now for the big question...

Why Computer Science?

Clearly I have a lot of varied interests, so why exactly did I end up in the field of computer science? Personally, I think the important aspect of all my activities is what they have in common and what characteristics kind of go together. I like being healthy, spending time/working with others, and helping people. Technology is a place where I can do all of these things in some form.

There are very few engineers who will ever say that they work alone, because engineering is a "team sport", so to speak. That's one of the aspects I love about it. Working with other people also satisfies my need for human interaction. And lets face it, who really wants to sit in a dark room starring into their computer screen all day? Definitely not me.

That brings us to the most important of all of this: helping others. I've grown up with people who have devoted their lives to the bettering of others. My parents, for example, gave up a lot to get my family where we are today. I want to be able to have someone say that about me, but more importantly: I want to do that for someone else. I believe that, through technology, I will be able to make the world a better place. It seems like every day something new and fancy is released; I want to be involved in that push for innovation and to help make technology more accesible and user friendly for all.

It may not be the most orthodox way, but hey! It works for me!
